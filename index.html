Maria Callas Fan Site!

Maria Callas is arguably the most influential soprano of the twentieth century in the operatic world. She launched her career in Greece and performed at the most prestigious opera houses around the globe ending her career in London, England. Her coloratura and bel canto styles are still held to be of the highest standards by many opera singers today.

Bio:

Maria Callas was a Greek-American born on December 2, 1923. She grew up in the United Stated and as a child moved to Greece and began her career during WW2. Her debut was in 1941 in Greece at the Royal Opera of Athens with the role Beatrice in the opera Boccaccio. The next year she was the lead in Tosca and her career took off from there. She went on to debut in Italy, Chicago, New York, London, Paris, and many other places. She died in her Parsian flat in 1977.
